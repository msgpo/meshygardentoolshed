Garden Mesh and Garden Gnodes

Garden Gnodes are very affordable, Wi-Fi enabled environmental sensors that are easy for anyone to use. Take charge of your garden by recording and logging real-time data from wherever you want!

Environmental sensors are nothing new, but unfortunately comparable system on the market is priced around $100 per unit, pricing them out of reach for most community gardeners and home gardeners in the United States and around the world.

By using the unique capabilities of the ESP8266 WI-FI processor and affordable, mass produced digital sensors, we have been able to build a unit that can measure the moisture level of the soil, humidity and temperature of the air, and the period and intensity of the light, in real-time, and send the data to any Wi-Fi enabled device, and all for only a few dollars per unit.

The Gnodes in a Garden Mesh do not need to be connected to the internet; the Garden Mesh forms a Wi-Fi network wherever it is deployed. The Gnodes can automatically communicate with any number of sister units within range. This means even "off grid" communities can take advantage of the full functionality of these sensors.

The "mesh" part of a Garden Mesh means that each unit can communicate through a daisy chain, to any other unit, giving it a unique capability as a kind of "walkie-talkie"; able to transmit text messages from any two Wi-Fi enabled devices connected to the mesh network.

Current necessary components:

  • ESP8266 flashed with NodeMCU firmware
  • Variety of enviromental sensors (currently DHT temperature/humidity sensor and capacitive soil moisture sensor)
  • Lua script to collect data from sensors and transmit it to MQTT broker
  • BabelD routing on OpenWrt routers
  • RaspberryPi to act as MQTT broker and HTTP server for hosting webpage with data

This project is currently based on the NodeMCU firmware.
